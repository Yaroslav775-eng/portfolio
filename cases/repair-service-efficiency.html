The Invisible Budget Burn

In local service industries—especially repair and emergency services—CPC (Cost Per Click) is notoriously high. This makes the account a primary target for competitor click fraud and low-quality broad-match queries. Our client was getting traffic, but their sales team was drowning in irrelevant calls and bot submissions that looked like real leads but never converted.

The "Hardened" Account Strategy

We didn't just optimize keywords; we built a defense perimeter. The goal was to ensure that every euro spent was a person with an actual broken appliance or a B2B repair request.

The 4-Layer Security Protocol

  • Layer 1: Competitor Exclusion. Custom scripts to identify and automatically exclude IP addresses demonstrating bot-like behavior or VPN traffic from competitor locations.
  • Layer 2: The "Brand Guard". A dedicated search perimeter ensuring competitor brands couldn't trigger ads for the client’s high-converting brand terms.
  • Layer 3: B2B Intent DSA. Dynamic Search Ads were limited to landing pages with high-value service content, filtering out DIY-ers looking for "how-to" advice.
  • Layer 4: Signal Verification. Only qualified leads (CRM verified) were fed back to the Google algorithm as conversions, training the bidding system to find actual customers.

The Transformation: Quality over Volume

By the end of the second month, the "junk" volume dropped by 65%. While the total click volume was lower, the qualified lead volume surged. The sales team began converting inquiries at a record rate because they were finally speaking to people who needed a professional service, not bots or competitors testing the ads. We held a steady €11.5 CPA across 1,000+ inquiries—a efficiency level previously thought impossible in this niche.
