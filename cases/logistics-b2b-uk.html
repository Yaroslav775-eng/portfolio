B2B Lead Generation

Cleaning Up UK B2B Logistics Demand and Doubling Qualified Lead Volume.

Executive Summary: This B2B logistics client was targeting companies looking for air freight and sea freight from China into the UK. At the start, lead volume was low, inquiries were expensive, tracking was incomplete, and parts of the budget were leaking into unqualified and bot-driven traffic. Over a 6-month engagement, I rebuilt the account around B2B Search, DSA, PMax, creative-led expansion, offline conversions from the CRM, and technical traffic cleanup. Result: qualified lead volume roughly doubled without increasing CPL, while a key reporting window delivered 1,090 qualified business inquiries at €11.5 per lead.

The Problem: Traffic existed, but sales did not trust the quality

The client operated in a complex B2B niche where advertising needed to generate more than form fills. It needed to bring in companies that genuinely required an international freight partner. At the start, the account was not producing a stable stream of commercially relevant inquiries. Lead volume was limited, lead cost was inconsistent, and the sales team had poor visibility into which campaigns were actually generating valuable B2B demand.

Tracking was another major weakness. The system could record a form submission, but it was not feeding Google a strong enough signal about which leads were truly valuable to the business. On top of that, there were recurring issues with low-quality traffic: unqualified inquiries, click fraud, and bot activity that distorted performance and diluted spend.

The Key Shift

The goal was not simply to add more traffic. The goal was to build a structure that separated real B2B demand from noise, trained the platform on lead quality, and gave sales a lead flow they could actually work with.

What Changed

The engagement lasted 6 months and moved across multiple layers at once: campaign structure, keyword strategy, creative support, tracking, CRM feedback, and technical traffic cleanup.

The New Operating Model

The account was rebuilt so each layer had one job: capture hot intent, surface long-tail B2B demand, expand relevant reach, and feed quality signals back from the CRM.

  • B2B Search as a filter, not a broad-reach layer. Keyword coverage was cleaned of consumer and mixed-intent queries. Search became a precision layer for commercial B2B demand.
  • Large-scale negative keyword cleanup. This removed a meaningful share of unqualified demand and reduced spend on traffic outside the client profile.
  • DSA for the long tail. DSA surfaced specific freight and shipping queries that are difficult to map manually, especially in a service-driven B2B category.
  • PMax and display creatives. Performance Max and creative-led support helped extend reach into relevant business audiences instead of relying only on existing search demand.
  • CRM feedback loop and offline conversions. Optimization moved beyond form fills and started learning from a better signal: whether the inquiry was actually sales-qualified.
  • Traffic cleanup. Click fraud, bot activity, and other low-value traffic sources were reduced so the budget could work on real commercial demand.

Why CRM Integration Mattered

In B2B, campaigns often drift toward the cheapest forms unless the platform is taught what a valuable lead actually looks like. Once offline conversion feedback was connected, Google Ads began learning from signals much closer to real lead quality. That made it possible to improve lead relevance without losing control of CPL.

This was the turning point that made the account more mature: advertising was no longer operating in isolation, but as part of a system connecting click, form, lead validation, and business value.

The Outcome: More Qualified Demand Without Inflating CPL

In the key reporting window, the campaigns delivered 1,090 qualified business inquiries at €11.5 per lead on roughly €10.3k in spend. Over the full 6-month engagement, qualified lead volume grew materially — roughly doubling — while lead cost remained stable.

For the sales team, the impact was larger than the headline metric. The funnel became cleaner, time was no longer wasted on obviously poor-fit inquiries, and paid traffic began to produce a more commercially relevant stream of leads.

What This Case Proved

  • B2B logistics needs demand filtration, not just reach.
  • DSA and PMax perform better when supported by a clean keyword base and clear campaign roles.
  • CRM and offline conversions are not optional extras in B2B. They are the foundation of better optimization.
  • Removing bots and click fraud can matter as much as creative or bidding work.
