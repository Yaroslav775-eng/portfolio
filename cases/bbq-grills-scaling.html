The Efficiency Trap

The client entered their most important sales window with a legacy account structure: 400+ SKUs clustered into a single, "flat" Shopping campaign. To the Google algorithm, a €15 grill cleaner was identical to a €2,000 professional smoker. The result? Budget gravitated toward cheap accessories with high click volume, while high-ticket grills—the real revenue drivers—remained underfunded.

The Strategic Pivot

Scaling e-commerce isn't about bidding higher; it's about bidding smarter. We abandoned the "all-in-one" approach and rebuilt the infrastructure around the business's actual bottom line.

The Margin-First Logic

  • Tier 1: High-Ticket Stars. Premium grills were moved to dedicated PMax campaigns with high ROAS targets, ensuring they received 70% of the total budget.
  • Tier 2: The Accessories Layer. Lower-margin items were moved to standard Shopping campaigns with low bids. They served as "add-on" revenue without draining the main budget.
  • Tier 3: Long-Tail DSA. Dynamic Search Ads were deployed to capture hyper-specific seasonal queries (e.g., "stainless steel offset smoker for balcony") that standard keywords often miss.

The Results: Data-Driven Scaling

By the height of the season, the account wasn't just spending more—it was spending better. GA4 data showed that the average order value (AOV) increased by 45% as our focus shifted to premium products. The final window produced €157k in revenue with a healthy 6.7x return on ad spend, allowing the client to reinvest with confidence.
